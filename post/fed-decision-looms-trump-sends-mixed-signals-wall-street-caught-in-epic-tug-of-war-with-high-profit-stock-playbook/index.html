I. When the Stock Market Becomes a Political Bargaining Chip: A White House-Directed "Scripted Drama"

(1) Trump’s Theatrics Are Splitting Wall Street

Don’t assume Wall Street is a sacred temple immune to politics. This week, all three major U.S. indexes danced like puppets tied to Trump’s teleprompter. On May 6, when the former president promised a “major announcement on Thursday, Friday, or next Monday,” S&P futures surged 1.2% instantly. Moments later, he added, “No progress on China trade,” sending the market plunging like a punctured balloon.

This Wall Street version of Rashomon plays out daily: the White House uses verbal reassurances, the Fed is forced to act as a stabilizing front, and the Treasury manipulates numbers behind the scenes. The most cunning move? A Thursday meeting announced between top U.S. and Chinese officials in Switzerland. Futures spiked 1.8% after hours—this “Swiss Waltz” alone gave the Dow an extra 200 points.

(2) The Fed’s Dilemma: Inflation Cross or Economic Guillotine

Nick Sullivan, often seen as the Fed’s unofficial spokesperson, has become a kind of performance artist lately. On one hand, he insists, “We will never bow to White House pressure,” while on the other, he hints that this week’s rate decision faces a “final showdown between inflation’s chokehold and economic decapitation.” This two-faced posture reflects deep anxiety inside the Fed: strong economic data is ripping apart any illusions of a rate cut.

Understanding the May decision means grasping the bull by the horns. Bloomberg data now shows a 98.7% probability of no rate cut in May—but June remains a dice roll. Powell’s wording will determine whether the S&P races to 6000 or crashes toward 5000. Wall Street is playing Russian roulette: bet on inflation to the left, recession to the right.

II. Quantum Trading in the Shadows: When Humans Meet AI Executioners

(1) Magical TD9 vs. Quant Machines—The Final Showdown

Palantir (PLTR) has become the battleground between chartists and quant funds. When the TD Sequential Indicator flashed a 9, the stock paused sharply—but that was just the beginning. The post-earnings plunge was driven not by humans, but by an AI-powered trading army responsible for 80% of volume.

These algorithmic “financial vultures” are rewriting market logic: they don’t care about fundamentals, only price curves; they ignore company narratives, focusing purely on volatility signals. When PLTR dove at the TD9 marker, it wasn’t due to a downgrade—it was because countless quant models triggered the same “trend reversal” flag simultaneously.

(2) UPST’s Collapse: Why a Blowout Quarter Got Punished

Upstart’s (UPST) earnings read like a textbook success: 67% YoY revenue growth, 96% narrower losses. Yet the stock was brutally sold off. Why? Because markets always trade the future.

When investors saw EBITDA margin shrink from 20% to 19% and Q2 revenue guidance grow just 3.8% sequentially, AI traders immediately triggered their “expectation adjustment” routines. The killer blow: despite the growth, the company remained unprofitable. That kind of quality is like gold paint on rotten wood—flashy on the outside, structurally weak inside.

III. Strategic Industry Battlegrounds: Who’s Quietly Repositioning in the Trade War?

(1) Nuclear Energy’s Stunning Reversal: CEG’s "Atomic Bomb" Move

While clean energy stocks slumped, Constellation Energy (CEG) staged a royal comeback. Shares had dropped 9% before earnings—then a White House announcement to accelerate nuclear reactor deployment flipped the narrative overnight. Futures jumped.

In a time of growing tariff tensions, nuclear stocks are becoming “bomb shelters”: immune to trade disputes (energy demand is inelastic) and benefiting from government subsidies. This double moat is attracting smart capital. More importantly, nuclear energy is evolving from a utility sector to a strategic emerging industry—a shift that explains the Axios-triggered rally.

(2) The Middle East Robotaxi War: PONY and WRD Go Full Throttle

Pony.ai’s (PONY) 47.6% single-day surge gave Wall Street a glimpse into the next decade’s tech war. The Chinese unicorn, teaming up with Uber, launched a robotaxi blitz in the Middle East—a full-on Normandy landing in smart mobility.

Meanwhile, WeRide (WRD) jumped 31.7% on news of plans to expand into 15 global cities within five years. This bold roadmap is firing up investors’ imaginations. As autonomous driving shifts from Silicon Valley labs to Middle Eastern streets, the tech war is at a boiling point—and the losers are those still obsessed with internal combustion KPIs.

IV. The Ultimate Code to Institutions vs. Retail: Who’s Pulling the Emotional Strings?

(1) Ford’s Double Agent Strategy

When Ford (F) withdrew its full-year guidance, most expected the stock to tank—but it rose instead. This is Wall Street psychology at its finest: smart money deliberately creates negative expectations to bait retail traders into buying the dip.

This “buy the rumor, sell the news” tactic is a classic market ploy. While retail panicked over a $1.5 billion tariff hit, institutions quietly accumulated shares—seeing long-term value in EV transformation and AI narratives. It’s like Tesla (TSLA): despite collapsing in Europe, its stock rose. In this market, fundamentals take a back seat to storytelling.

(2) The AMD vs. NVIDIA Chess Match

AMD’s earnings beat sparked a rally, revealing deep shifts in industry dynamics. The perennial number two is now challenging NVIDIA’s (NVDA) throne—57% growth in data center revenue is no fluke. This signals a rules rewrite in the compute revolution.

Behind this silent war is a brutal truth: as AI models devour global compute power, chipmakers must choose—follow or disrupt. AMD’s stock surge is not just a nod to good numbers, but a preview of its bid for narrative dominance. Wall Street is witnessing a live-action version of The Three-Body Problem.

V. U.S. Equities at a Crossroads: The Next 90 Days Are Do-or-Die

(1) TD9’s Final Warning

With the S&P 500 posting two consecutive doji candles, the market is clearly gridlocked. Tech analysts see resistance at 5700–5800, but the greater risk lies with the TD9 signal—a potential countdown to detonation.

Historically, a TD9 trigger results in a “false reversal” about 30% of the time. This means the current tug-of-war could explode into a full-blown collapse. Investors need to be like leopards now—those positioning early must beware of getting reversed on, and latecomers must tighten their stops.

(2) June Rate Cut Roulette

Powell’s tone will be the Damocles sword hanging over the market. If he leans hawkish, expect an epic crash; if dovish, we could ignite another bull run.

This binary risk is fueling new arbitrage plays: smart money is now long volatility and Treasuries, while retail swings between FOMO and panic. This market drama—staged by politics, economics, and algorithms—will reach its final act in the next three weeks.
