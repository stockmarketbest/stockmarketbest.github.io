U.S. Stocks Surge: Tech Rebounds, AI Fever Spreads, Tariff Clouds Linger—Heaven or Hell Ahead?

2025-03-15 12 min read

I. The U.S. Market’s Comeback: Tech Stocks Reborn, AI as the Savior

1.1 Market Rejuvenation: All Major Indexes Rally

After a turbulent week of relentless declines, U.S. stocks staged a robust rebound on Friday, March 14. The S&P 500 jumped over 2%, the Nasdaq rallied by a similar margin, and the Dow Jones Industrial Average climbed 1.65%. All three major indexes turned positive in what resembled a long-awaited downpour after a drought.
Yet, can this rebound truly ease investor anxieties? The S&P 500 still recorded a 2.27% decline for the week—its fourth consecutive drop. The recent technical bounce, born from oversold conditions, might only be a temporary release; if the rebound overshoots, the risk of a sharp correction remains ever-present.

1.2 Tech Stocks Rewrite Their Fate: AI Sparks a Revival

At the heart of the rally were the major technology stocks—especially those in the chip sector—propelled by renewed investor confidence in AI. AI has emerged as the unexpected hero, reviving sentiment overnight.
Notably, Foxconn—one of Nvidia’s key suppliers—dismissed recent reports of slowing AI demand. Despite a quarterly profit dip, the company forecasted a “significant revenue surge” this quarter, fueled by strong demand from tech clients. Its chairman dismissed any talk of a slowdown, predicting that first-quarter AI server revenue would double year-over-year and month-over-month, eventually accounting for more than half of the company’s server income. In a related twist, Intel welcomed its first Chinese CEO, signaling a do-or-die approach. With AMD’s past turnaround echoing in the background, all eyes are now on Nvidia’s upcoming GTC conference, as investors hope for a major breakthrough to reinvigorate a sluggish market.

1.3 A Rare Moment of Quiet from Trump

Adding to the market’s relief was an unusual break in controversy: President Trump notably refrained from his typical barrage on social media. Without the barrage of tariff threats, the market could finally catch its breath. The old adage “when Trump is silent, it’s a good day” may sound crude, yet it rings true in these volatile times. However, questions remain about how long this “stolen calm” can last.

II. The Tariff Shadow: Crumbling Consumer Confidence and the Inflation Demon Awakening

2.1 Consumer Confidence: From Paradise to Despair

Despite the exuberance in tech stocks, underlying uncertainties persist. Tariff policies have cast a long shadow, sharply denting consumer confidence. A recent survey by the University of Michigan showed that U.S. consumer confidence fell from 64.7 in February to 57.9 in March—a two-year low and well below the expected 63.1.
This steep drop signals that consumers are increasingly anxious about their financial future, with expectations sinking to record lows. The ominous cloud of tariffs not only undermines confidence but also hints at even larger economic challenges ahead.

2.2 The Inflation Demon Resurges

Equally alarming are the runaway inflation expectations. One-year inflation forecasts surged from 4.3% to 4.9%, the highest level since 2022, while five-year expectations leaped from 3.5% to 3.9%—a peak not seen in over three decades. The moment these figures were released, gains in the major indexes shrank, nearly derailing the rebound.
Once the inflation beast is unleashed, the consequences could be dire. Plummeting consumer confidence combined with escalating inflation expectations may well plant a time bomb in the economy.

2.3 A Tug-of-War: Confidence Versus Panic

Despite these sobering indicators, the AI-fueled rally managed to push the indexes higher. The initial panic following the data release was soon overshadowed by the robust gains in tech stocks. This tug-of-war reveals a stark truth: the market is currently a battlefield where confidence and fear are in constant conflict. Ultimately, next week’s key events may determine which side prevails.

III. Technical Rebound or a Fleeting Miracle? The Market at a Crossroads

3.1 Oversold Bounce: A Technical Breather

The S&P 500 managed to climb back above its five-day moving average—a modest technical recovery after a severe oversell due to rapid declines. Ahead of next week’s pivotal events (Nvidia’s GTC conference and the Federal Reserve meeting), some short-sellers have taken profits, sparking a short squeeze.
Traders are now closely watching resistance levels around the 200-day moving average, notably at 5750 and 5850 points. Should the market fail to break through or sustain above these levels, a further drop could prompt a swift return of the bears.

3.2 Deep V-Reversal? Don’t Hold Your Breath

Given the lingering tariff uncertainties, a deep V-reversal to the previous high of 6147 points (reached on February 19) seems unlikely. Data from CFRA Research suggests that after over a 10% drop without entering bear market territory, the S&P 500 has historically taken an average of eight months to recover its previous highs. At this rate, the 6147 mark may not be regained until mid-October.
That said, recent market cycles in the U.S. have been marked by rapid turnarounds, with 10-15% drops often reversing quickly—unless, of course, a true bear market sets in.

3.3 Next Week’s Showdown: GTC and the Fed Hold the Key

Next week is set to be a decisive period. The outcomes of Nvidia’s GTC conference and the Federal Reserve meeting will likely determine whether the current market euphoria persists or if the market tumbles back into despair.

IV. Liquidity and Valuation: Uncovering the Deep-Rooted Issues in the U.S. Market Decline

4.1 Liquidity: Underneath the Façade of Ease

There are growing concerns that market liquidity may not hold up. Since the Fed’s quantitative tightening (QT) began in mid-2022, the central bank has reduced its balance sheet by over $2 trillion, though it still remains well above pre-pandemic levels. U.S. bank reserves now stand at approximately $3.2 trillion. While liquidity appears ample on the surface, warning signs of strain are emerging.
For instance, overnight reverse repo balances have fallen to $126.2 billion from their previous highs and are expected to be fully drawn down in the second quarter. If the Fed continues its QT efforts after this point, U.S. stocks could face severe pressure.

4.2 Valuation Correction: The Cost of an Overheated Market

This recent downturn can be seen as a necessary correction to “kill valuations.” In recent years, the S&P 500’s forward price-to-earnings ratio soared to 23 times, but it has since been trimmed down to 19.5 times—below the five-year average of 19.8, yet still above the ten-year average of 18.3.
If the economy avoids a full-blown recession and only experiences a mild slowdown, a 19.5 ratio might be sustainable in a low-interest environment. However, if a recession takes hold, a ratio closer to 16 could be the realistic floor. The market is currently trading on the anticipation of a recession rather than its actual onset. A shift in sentiment could lead to a quicker-than-expected rebound.

4.3 The Hidden Killer: Rising Neutral Interest Rates

Another less obvious but significant threat is the rising neutral interest rate. Often referred to as a “hidden killer,” this factor limits the potential for valuation expansion. Combined with liquidity constraints and heightened interest rate pressures, the path back to market highs becomes increasingly challenging.

V. Stock Winners and Pitfalls: Who Will Emerge and Who Will Falter?

5.1 Nvidia-Related Stocks: Speculation or Substance?

Stocks tied to Nvidia have soared, but not all are built on solid fundamentals. For example, WRD, an autonomous driving firm, reported disappointing financial results: fourth-quarter revenue of CNY 140 million (a 2.76% year-over-year decline) and an adjusted loss of CNY 250 million. Its forecast for 2024—a revenue drop of 10% and a deeper net loss—underscores the speculative nature of these “Nvidia-boosted” plays. Should Nvidia’s influence wane, these stocks could quickly turn to worthless paper.

5.2 Quantum ‘Fraud’ Stocks: A Spectacle or a Pitfall?

Quantum computing stocks experienced a frenzy today, with QBTS surging by 47% and several others posting double-digit gains. Next week’s “Quantum Day” at GTC will put these stocks in the spotlight, as Nvidia seeks to make amends with the market. However, while they might offer a bit of speculative fun, their fundamentals leave much to be desired.

5.3 Ulta Beauty: Impressive Earnings with Hidden Caveats

Ulta Beauty’s earnings report was a bright spot, with fourth-quarter revenue reaching $3.5 billion—a slight 1.9% decline that still met expectations—and an EPS of $8.46, surpassing forecasts. However, the outlook for fiscal 2025 was less rosy, with revenue guidance of $11.5–11.6 billion (just below the expected $11.7 billion) and EPS guidance of $22.5–22.9 (compared to an expected $23.51). The stock’s rally appears to be riding on the current market bounce, but weaker guidance could become a drag if conditions worsen.

VI. Where Do Markets Go from Here? Paradise or Perdition?

6.1 Short-Term Euphoria, Long-Term Uncertainty

While the recent rebound has provided the market with a brief respite, persistent challenges—including tariff uncertainties, plummeting consumer confidence, and runaway inflation expectations—loom large. The upcoming GTC conference and Fed meeting will be crucial in determining whether the market surges towards new highs or plunges into deeper trouble.

6.2 Investment Advice: Seize Opportunities with Caution

Investors should maintain a clear head. While chasing tech stocks in the short term might offer gains, setting stop-loss orders is essential to manage risk. Over the medium to long term, the threats posed by tariffs and inflation remain significant. Balancing the allure of the AI boom with vigilance against potential downturns is key to surviving this volatile market environment.

6.3 Future Outlook: Accelerated Recovery or Onset of a Bear Market?

If the economy merely slows rather than slips into recession, the rebound could occur sooner than expected. Conversely, if a bear market truly sets in, reclaiming the previous high of 6147 points may prove to be a distant dream. All eyes now turn to next week, which could very well be the decisive turning point.

Conclusion: A Ship Battling Stormy Seas

The U.S. stock market is navigating tumultuous waters—rocking between a tech-fueled rally and the persistent threat of tariffs. Is the AI surge a genuine lifeline or just a mirage? Are inflation and crumbling consumer confidence minor ripples, or do they portend a catastrophic tidal wave? This in-depth analysis, though expansive, only scratches the surface of the underlying challenges. In such an unpredictable environment, only careful analysis and decisive action will allow investors to emerge victorious in this high-stakes game.
