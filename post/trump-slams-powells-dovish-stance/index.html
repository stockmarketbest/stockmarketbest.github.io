Trump Slams Powell's Dovish Stance

2025-05-08 11 min read

# QQQ # SPY # DIA # .DJI # .SPX # .IXIC # GLD # BTC # TLT # trump # stock market # NVDA # TSLA # AAPL # META # GOOGL # MSFT # AMZN

Hello everyone, it’s Wednesday, May 7, and the U.S. stock market delivered yet another dizzying rollercoaster. Trump and Powell took center stage with a barrage of headlines, while bulls and bears slugged it out like amateurs in a backyard brawl. By the close, the major indexes managed a tiny gain, but the tepid moves hardly lived up to the hype of an FOMC decision day. Why? The announcement and Powell’s press conference were long-winded and devoid of fresh substance—more of a snooze-fest than a market mover.

But don’t mistake this surface calm for true tranquility. Trump lit the fuse with tariff threats and trade talk, spooking investors. Powell, meanwhile, played his usual verbal tai chi—neither dovish nor hawkish enough to inspire confidence. Gold bounced on India-Pakistan tensions, while names like Google took hits from Apple’s dagger, Nvidia caught a breath on relaxed policy, and Disney, UBER, and SMCI each brought their own drama. All in all, it was a nauseating ride on the financial rollercoaster.

I. Market Recap: Gains Squeezed Like Toothpaste

1. U.S. Indices: Small Gains Mask Bigger Worries

The S&P 500 hovered near the flatline for most of the session, too timid to make a meaningful move. At 2 p.m., the Fed held rates steady at 4.25%-4.5% with no change to the pace of balance sheet runoff. The statement flagged “increased uncertainty” for the economic outlook, noted a “resilient” labor market, but also acknowledged rising risks around unemployment and inflation.

Markets reacted as if they had just popped a sedative—everything was priced in, no surprises either way. Fed funds futures traders remained firm in their bets for three cuts this year, starting in July. But the calm didn’t last. Trump’s tariff comments sent the indices lower, only to be partially rescued by late-session news on AI chip policy relaxations. The final uptick felt more like toothpaste being squeezed out of a near-empty tube—hardly comforting in an anxious market.

2. VIX & Technicals: Underlying Tensions Simmer

The VIX stayed calm around 24 heading into the decision, signaling traders weren’t scrambling to hedge. Technically, the S&P 500 has printed three straight doji candles, caught between the 50-day and 250-day moving averages. Breakout? Unlikely. Sideways churn seems far more probable in the short term. Intraday traders are getting whipsawed, while long-term investors may as well sit back and sip tea.

3. Sentiment Snapshot: Whiplash from Mixed Signals

From Trump’s aggressive rhetoric to Powell’s vague reassurances to a late-day tech policy twist, the headlines came fast and furious. Bulls and bears were both dazed. Long-term investors can afford patience; day traders are likely questioning their life choices.

II. Trump’s Verbal Grenades: Tariff Talk Rattles Markets

1. One Comment, One Dive

Trump stirred the pot at a U.S. ambassador’s swearing-in ceremony, declaring he had no interest in removing China’s 145% tariffs and sarcastically remarking that “China should reconsider who initiated the talks.” The market read this as: “You came to the table, not me.” Stocks immediately took a dive, with investor nerves stretched to the limit.

2. The Real Game: Politics Over Economics

Trump’s tough talk may appear targeted at Beijing, but the real audience is back home. He’s playing strongman to energize his base, reminding voters he's still willing to take on China. But is he blind to the economic fallout? Of course not. Tariffs at 145% jack up input costs for U.S. firms and drain consumers’ wallets. It’s a lose-lose. But Trump doesn’t care—he’s counting votes, not dollars.

3. Market Impact: Short-Term Panic, Long-Term Drag

In the short run, Trump’s words dashed hopes for any weekend trade progress. Most expect little more than diplomatic niceties. Long term, if tariffs stay, U.S.-China supply chains will remain fractured. American manufacturers and tech firms will feel the squeeze, while China prepares to hunker down. This geopolitical time bomb is far from defused.

III. Powell’s Verbal Tai Chi: Long on Words, Short on Clarity

1. Speech Content: All Talk, No Signal

Powell’s post-decision remarks boiled down to this: the economy is strong, labor is solid, inflation is falling, policy effects may be “transitory,” and the Fed won’t preemptively cut but stands ready to act.

Translation? Nothing new. Just a lot of hedging. Markets hate this kind of ambiguity. Indexes swung wildly during his presser, as bulls and bears volleyed with no clear winner.

2. Hawk or Dove? Powell Plays Both Sides

At first blush, Powell sounded dovish, especially calling inflation “transitory.” But he also rejected the idea of preemptive cuts, clearly wary of inflation rebounding. He’s boxed in—can’t sound too hawkish and upset the White House, can’t go too dovish and risk reigniting inflation. So he talks like a dove, acts like a hawk, and leaves markets grasping at straws.

3. Déjà Vu: Market Doesn’t Trust Him Anymore

The kicker? Powell once again used the “transitory” label on inflation—the same one he clung to in 2021-22, right before inflation ran rampant. Investors have long memories. Many are thinking: “Come on, Jay, not this again…”

IV. U.S.-China Trade Talks: Don’t Expect Fireworks

1. Tough Talk All Around

Trump’s hardline stance is crystal clear. China’s unlikely to fold easily either. This weekend’s talks will be more about reading each other than reaching breakthroughs. Markets already smell a dead-end.

2. Outlook: Wait-and-See in the Short Run, Strategic Chess Long-Term

Short term, markets won’t expect much. The focus will be on post-meeting statements—whether Trump turns diplomatic or lights another fuse. Long term, this trade chess game will drag on for years. Both sides want balance, neither wants to blink first.

3. Market Mood: Uncertainty Reigns Supreme

The worst thing for investors is not knowing. With no clear signal from trade talks, capital remains cautious. Until something decisive happens, the sideways grind continues.

V. Gold Pops on India-Pakistan Flare-Up

1. Price Action: A Geo-Political Boost

Renewed military tension between India and Pakistan gave gold a boost, nearly erasing its recent losses. As usual, when geopolitical tensions rise, so does the demand for safe havens.

2. Outlook: Fed Holds the Key

Gold’s ability to hold this rebound depends heavily on the Fed. If Powell leans dovish and rate cut expectations firm up, real yields drop—and gold gains ground. The market’s still betting on a July cut, so there’s short-term upside.

3. Narrative: Risk Aversion Still Rules

At the end of the day, gold is a proxy for global fear. Whether it’s war or economic wobble, gold benefits from chaos. India-Pakistan tensions are minor for now, but escalation could spark a much bigger rally.

VI. Stock Spotlight: Tech Carnage and Comebacks

1. Google: Backstabbed by Apple

Google got wrecked today, with shares plunging mid-session like it slipped on a banana peel. Apple’s plan to integrate AI search into Safari—and potentially work with OpenAI—was the dagger. It’s a direct challenge to Google’s search dominance. The stock broke down toward April 7 support and may breach $135.

Despite its strong cash flow, Google is under pressure. Antitrust heat, AI disruption, and looming ad softness in H2 could keep bears circling. Wall Street sharks smell blood, and they don’t let go.

2. Apple: Playing It Lazy, Winning Anyway

Apple hurt Google but didn’t walk away unscathed—its stock slipped too. Google pays Apple $20 billion a year to be Safari’s default search engine, about 15% of Apple’s profit. Now Apple hints it might ditch Google, work with Bing, and bolt on an AI assistant.

In short: Apple wants to use others’ tech and take a cut—earning without building. Smart in the short term, but risky if it falls behind in AI innovation.

3. NVDA: Bounces on Policy News

Nvidia surged late on reports of loosened AI chip restrictions. But don’t cheer just yet—Washington’s unlikely to fully lift export limits to China. The upside is modest, with long-term sales gains capped. Nvidia still needs stronger catalysts to break its downtrend.

4. AppLovin: Wild Earnings Ride

AppLovin’s Q1 earnings triggered a wild post-market ride—first down 8%, then up 13%. The V-shaped recovery came as strong profits and revenue beat expectations, though Q2 ad guidance fell short. Investors shrugged off the miss, betting this under-owned AI name has room to run.

5. Disney: Beats Big, Spirits High

Disney crushed it this quarter—EPS of $1.45 trounced $1.20 estimates, sending shares up 10%. Theme parks and streaming both outperformed, and the company raised full-year guidance to $5.75 EPS. CEO Bob Iger also talked up cruise growth over the next 3–4 years. Recession? Disney’s not sweating it—entertainment remains essential.

6. UBER: Rebound After Dip

UBER’s Q1 report prompted a sharp drop at the open—down 6.5%—but it clawed back most losses, closing just 2.5% lower. Revenue missed slightly, but EPS of $0.83 crushed the $0.51 forecast. Bookings guidance beat, too. Still, the rally might take a breather after this.

7. SMCI: Struggling to Catch a Break

SMCI has been battered since last week’s warning. Yesterday’s earnings cut full-year guidance, blaming tariffs and economic headwinds. Shares dropped again today, only saved by the chip policy headlines. Near-term outlook is grim; Q3 will be a real test.

VII. Final Thoughts: Stay Sharp in Choppy Waters

A volatile day driven by Trump’s threats, Powell’s hedging, and murky trade talk. U.S. stocks barely closed higher, hiding deep-seated anxiety. Gold found footing on geopolitical fears, while big tech saw bloodshed. Traders are exhausted.

What’s next? Uncertainty dominates. Trade talks lack promise, Fed policy is unclear, and markets are stuck grinding sideways. Smart investors need to stay vigilant—ignore surface-level moves. Opportunities are there, but so are minefields. Survival in this market requires one thing: caution.
