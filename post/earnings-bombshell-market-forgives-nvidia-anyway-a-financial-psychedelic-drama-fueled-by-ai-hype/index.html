Earnings Bombshell? Market Forgives NVIDIA Anyway – A Financial Psychedelic Drama Fueled by AI Hype

1. Let’s Be Honest—This Isn’t an Earnings Report. It’s an AI Faith Rally!

May 28th—NVIDIA dropped what they called an “earnings report.” Was it impressive? Sure, some numbers pop: 69% revenue growth, 73% data center revenue growth, even gaming up 42%. But scratch the surface, and the cracks show: earnings per share missed expectations, and their Q2 outlook? Underwhelming, with a brutal $8 billion drop in H20 chip revenue.

In other words, this “report” is like a beautifully wrapped trash can—looks nice, but open it up, and you’ll find the same old mess. Yet, what does the market do? Shrugs it off and throws a forgiveness party—NVIDIA stock rockets 6% in after-hours trading.

You can almost hear Jensen Huang’s sly grin: “Oh, you want AI hype? I’ll give you AI hype. Growth? Here, have some growth.”

It’s ironic—earlier this year, even a perfect beat-and-raise would’ve been torn apart by the market’s nitpicking. But now? As long as NVIDIA wears that shiny AI halo, they get a free pass.

This isn’t an earnings season. It’s an AI cult revival meeting.

2. The Bond Market Is a Ticking Time Bomb—How Long Can Stocks Ride This Wave?

Don’t just stare at NVIDIA’s fireworks. Look behind the curtain: the bond market is already on fire. Japan’s long-term bond auctions? Disaster after disaster—40-year bonds see the weakest demand since July, 20-year bonds also flopped, triggering a global long-bond selloff that even rattled FX markets.

Over in the US? JPMorgan data shows short positions in Treasuries hitting a new high since February, while net longs drop to multi-month lows. Translation: the market is sending a loud, clear message—we don’t trust US debt, and we sure as hell don’t trust the Fed.

This bond market turbulence is bleeding into equities: AI hype is propping up stocks, but liquidity tightening and rising yields are lurking like a guillotine overhead. Think you can just ride this rally forever? Don’t kid yourself. Markets run on sentiment, and when the bubble starts to leak, no amount of faith can stop the collapse.

Look at NVIDIA’s Q2 guide—below expectations, and this during peak AI euphoria. If the market darling is already warning of softer growth, what do you think that says for the rest of the tech sector? When NVIDIA’s myth finally cracks, what’s left to hold up the market? Meme stocks? Crypto? Speculative fads? It’s a game of musical chairs—when the music stops, someone’s left holding the bag, and it won’t be pretty.

3. Geopolitics, AI, and the Tech Cold War: The Real Game Behind NVIDIA’s Hype

Let’s not forget the elephant in the room: the US-China tech war.

The H20 chip ban? Originally estimated to cost NVIDIA $5.5 billion, now it’s “only” $4.5 billion—woohoo, what a win, right? Not really. This is just accounting gymnastics. The long-term trend is clear: NVIDIA’s China business is slowly being choked off. Meanwhile, Trump is cozying up to Middle Eastern oil money, locking in huge AI orders, while the US Commerce Department quietly tightens the noose—cutting off China’s access to chip design software and sabotaging its AI ambitions.

NVIDIA? Playing both sides like a pro. On the surface, they’re like, “Oh no, we’d love to sell to China, but Uncle Sam won’t let us.” Behind the scenes, they’re smirking: “No worries, we’ll just cook up an even more nerfed version of the H20 and keep selling you scraps.”

And China’s domestic chip players? Honestly, if they don’t step up their game, they’ll be stuck paying top dollar for castrated chips, groveling for leftovers. The AI war is no longer just a business rivalry—it’s a geopolitical chess match. If China doesn’t achieve chip independence fast, it’ll be forever playing defense, buying “crippled” products while the US dictates the rules.

4. The Great Speculation Game: Hot Stocks, Cold Reality

Let’s talk about the rest of the market:

  • CDNS? Got slammed.
  • C3.ai? Beat earnings, popped, but it’s just a hype trade.
  • HPQ? Terrible results, cutting guidance, hiking prices to survive—pure desperation.
  • Tempus AI? Crushed by a Spruce Point short report questioning everything from product viability to sketchy accounting—down 19%.
  • GME? Dipping into Bitcoin speculation—buying 4,710 BTC instead of building an actual business.
  • DJT? Just another hype vehicle.
  • Joby? Riding Toyota’s investment wave, but let’s be real: this is a speculative moonshot, waiting for the next liquidity flood to pump it.

It’s the same old story—concepts over fundamentals, sizzle over steak. Most of these companies are empty shells, fueled by hot money and the dream that “maybe the next pop is mine.”

It’s like a nightclub at 2 AM. Everyone knows they’re not there looking for a lifelong partner—they’re chasing a vibe, a thrill, maybe a quick win. Stocks are no different: we all know it’s risky, but we keep coming back for the rush. Just don’t forget—when the music stops, the lights come on, and reality hits, you’re the one holding the empty bag.

5. The AI Hype: Glitz and Glam with Cracks Beneath

AI is the sexiest story in town—ChatGPT, Sora, Grok, Gemini—names that sell the dream of an automated, frictionless future. Self-driving cars, auto-trading, content generation, robot overlords—you name it.

But the harsh reality? AI’s path to monetization is razor-thin. NVIDIA is the rare exception, raking it in as the pick-and-shovel provider. Most AI plays? They’re a game of smoke and mirrors—hype machines with little to no real revenue.

Even NVIDIA isn’t invincible. Its revenue stream relies heavily on a few big customers, who themselves are exposed to macro shocks, regulatory clampdowns, and geopolitical landmines. This is a high-wire act on an increasingly frayed rope.

For retail investors? You’re not the scalpel—you’re the lab rat. Most of the time, you won’t even see the blade until it’s too late. Chasing AI today, chasing semiconductors tomorrow, chasing flying cars the day after. It’s a cycle of chasing, chasing, chasing—while the insiders cash out, and the story changes.

6. Final Thoughts: Don’t Just Watch the Circus—Understand the Game

At the end of the day, the market’s “forgiveness” of NVIDIA isn’t really forgiveness—it’s a desperate gambler’s bet. As long as the AI narrative holds, everyone stays seated at the table, because leaving means admitting you missed the boat, and no one wants that FOMO.

This whole show is a cocktail of hype, liquidity, geopolitics, and retail FOMO. NVIDIA is just one star in the cast, but we’re all part of this play—some holding the script, some just cannon fodder.

Don’t fool yourself into thinking earnings reports explain the market. They don’t. The market runs on narratives, not numbers. Whoever spins the best story wins—until they don’t.

In this bubble-driven market, the only real strategy is staying awake. Don’t buy the hype. Don’t hitch your future to any single company’s promises. And remember: anyone who gets deified will eventually get destroyed.
