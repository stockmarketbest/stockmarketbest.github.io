Wave 5 is Here! The Market is Soaring—Big Boss Grins Evilly—Dare to Jump In?

2025-05-28 11 min read

# QQQ # SPY # DIA # .DJI # .SPX # .IXIC # GLD # BTC # trump # stock market # NVDA # TSLA # AAPL # META # GOOGL # MSFT # AMZN

Hey everyone! It’s Monday, May 27, 2025—right after the long weekend, and the market feels like it’s on steroids! The optimism is off the charts. U.S. stocks, the dollar, and treasuries are all pumping, while gold is getting crushed. The three major indices opened higher and kept climbing: the S&P 500 and Nasdaq jumped over 2%, and the Dow surged 1.78%. It’s a full-blown rally—everything’s soaring except gold. This vibe feels just like the start of a Wave 5 takeoff! But whether you make money depends on whether you dare to get on board—and whether you know when to get off. Let’s break down what’s happening and how to play this “evil Wave 5” like a pro!

1. The Market’s on Fire: Policy Eases, Sentiment Explodes

1. Policy Gifts Boost Confidence Big Time

Over the weekend, the news cycle went wild: Trump suddenly announced a delay on the 50% tariffs against the EU, pushing the deadline to July 9. White House economic advisor Hassett added that some tariffs might be cut to 10% or lower, with multiple trade deals coming this week. What’s this? A giant gift package for the markets! With policy risks easing, investor confidence shot through the roof—how could the market not rally?

2. Indices Soar—Who Dares to Short?

All three major indices are flashing green today. The S&P 500 gapped up and reclaimed its 5-day and 10-day moving averages, bulls are in full control. The VIX volatility index plummeted—traders aren’t even buying protective puts anymore! In this mood, who’d dare to short? Bears risk getting steamrolled by this runaway train.

3. Retail Investors Are Pumped, Institutions Still Sharpening Knives

There’s $7 trillion in cash still waiting on the sidelines. Retail investors have been buying hand over fist, but institutions aren’t fully committed yet. CTAs are neutral, hedge funds and mutual funds are underweight in tech. What does this mean? Institutions still have plenty of ammo left. If Nvidia’s earnings tomorrow deliver, the FOMO (fear of missing out) wave could erupt, and Wave 5’s main surge might ignite!

2. Bond Market Breathes, Global Equities Party

1. Japan’s Bond Market: Supply Cut to Calm the Storm

Japan’s Ministry of Finance saw ultra-long bond yields spike to historic highs and rushed to announce issuance cuts—a move to calm the markets. Yields promptly retreated, giving investors a breather. There’s more discussion coming in June—keep an eye on it.

2. U.S. Treasuries: Holding the Line

U.S. 30-year yields dipped below 5%, and today’s $69 billion 2-year Treasury auction went smoothly. There’s more supply coming tomorrow and the next day—demand will shape the outlook for Treasuries and the dollar. As Wall Street likes to say: “When policymakers panic, the market stops panicking.” With the bond market steady, global equities are partying. The logic is simple.

3. Sentiment Spillover: From Bonds to Stocks

A stable bond market acts like a shot of adrenaline for stocks. The global rebound is gaining steam. But don’t forget—this wave of optimism is fueled by both policy and sentiment. Whether it’s real growth or a mirage depends on the data that follows.

3. Consumer Confidence Soars: The Economy Isn’t That Bad

1. Blowout Data, Surging Optimism

U.S. consumer confidence for May jumped from 85.7 to 98, crushing all economist estimates (82–92.8). It’s not just the current mood—future expectations are optimistic too. Inflation expectations dropped from 5.9% to 5.3%—people aren’t as freaked out about price hikes anymore.

2. Hard Data and Trade Deals Fuel the Fire

Since early May, economic data has been solid, and Q1 earnings crushed expectations. The May 12 U.S.-China interim agreement boosted confidence even more. 44% of consumers expect the market to rise in the next year (up from 37.6% in April), while bearish sentiment dropped from 47.2% to 37.7%. That’s a clear bullish shift!

3. Market Expectations: Retail Leading the Charge

Rising consumer confidence directly lifts market expectations. Retail investors are charging ahead, while institutions sharpen their knives in the background. This setup screams Wave 5—if it doesn’t soar now, when will it?

4. Wave 5 Ignites: Grins Hide Danger

1. Technicals: Bulls Sharpen Their Swords

The S&P 500 has reclaimed its 5-day and 10-day moving averages, gapped up, and looks like Wave 4’s correction is over. If the next day or two hold these levels, Wave 5 is likely underway. Technical traders are drooling over these candles—it’s go time for the bulls!

2. Sentiment: Mania or Trap?

The VIX is tanking, the market is turning fully bullish, and retail investors are euphoric—while institutions are eerily calm. Remember, Wave 5 is the tail end of the uptrend—it's intense, but also dangerous. Historically, this phase rallies harder than you can believe—then reverses so brutally it’ll make you want to cry. With sentiment this hyped, risks are lurking everywhere.

3. Cash Waiting: $7 Trillion on Standby

That $7 trillion in sidelined cash hasn’t fully entered the market yet, and institutions have plenty of room to add. If Nvidia’s earnings deliver, cash could flood in, pushing indices even higher. But don’t forget—when retail goes all-in, big players might be quietly unloading at the top.

5. Stock Breakdown: Who Wins, Who Loses?

1. Nvidia: AI Bellwether—Can It Hold Up?

Nvidia plans to launch lower-tier AI chips for China in June, priced at $6,500–8,000. They’re cheaper, but performance is limited. Tomorrow’s earnings are crucial—Bank of America says Q1 could beat expectations, but Q2 guidance is risky due to policy issues. For Wave 5 to soar, Nvidia must hold strong. If it stumbles, the entire market will wobble.

2. PDD: Brutal Selloff, No Surprise

Pinduoduo’s Q1 revenue was $9.5 billion, missing estimates of $10.3 billion. Profit dropped 50%, and marketing costs surged 43%. Shares plunged 18% intraday, closing down 13.6%. Honestly, this beating is deserved. Chinese stocks have long disconnected from fundamentals—PDD’s just a speculative play, not a value investment.

3. Apple: India to the Rescue—Just Keep Trump Quiet

Apple shipped 3 million iPhones in India in April, up 76%. The stock rebounded 2.53%. Morgan Stanley says even if a 25% tariff hits, overseas production still has a cost advantage, and a 4%-6% price hike could absorb it. If the market holds and Trump doesn’t throw a tantrum, Apple’s got room to run.

4. DJT: King of Hype, Truth or Fiction?

DJT (Trump’s media stock) was rumored to issue $1.5 billion in stock + $1 billion in convertible bonds to buy Bitcoin. Premarket, the stock surged 16%—then crashed 10.4% at close. The company said it was “fake news,” then actually announced the offering today. This thing’s a shell—a gambler’s playground. Fundamentals? Who cares. It’s a pure hype machine.

5. AMC: Box Office Boost, Rebound Play

AMC set a Memorial Day weekend box office record, with the stock up 24%—rebounding over 50% from its lows. Big films and crowds are back. It’s testing the 250-day moving average—if it holds, a reversal could stick; otherwise, the 200-day offers support. Short-term, it’s a spectacle; mid-term, it’s a trend watch.

6. UNH: Defensive Giant, Lurking Quietly

UNH, the defensive play, lags when markets are roaring. When the hype fades, that’s when this old-timer shines. Hold tight—long-term, it’s solid as a rock.

6. Strategy: Score Big or Get Burned?

1. Trend: Wave 5 Is a Beast—But the Tail’s a Knife

Wave 5 rallies like crazy—but it’s the end of the cycle. After this, we could see an ABC crash. The size of the rally is unpredictable—it might even surpass Wave 3. But the more euphoric it gets, the harsher the reversal. Don’t let the big boss’s evil grin blind you.

2. Tactics: Don’t Fight the Trend, Control Risk

  • Don’t short: Shorting now is asking to get crushed. This momentum is a freight train—stand in the way, and you’ll get flattened.
  • Don’t chase highs: After several days of big gains, take profits in chunks. Don’t wait until the reversal to regret it.
  • Play the index: If you’re unsure about stocks, just buy the index and ride the wave—it’s the safest way to lock in gains.

3. Stock Picks: Stick to Leaders, Avoid Weaklings

Blue chips and leaders drive Wave 5—small caps lag. Don’t be that person stuck in a dud while the index flies. Watch policies and fundamentals—don’t get swept up in retail hype.

7. Final Take: Jump In Fast, Exit Hard

This market feels like the big boss giving you a wicked grin—full of temptation, hiding danger. Wave 5 is here, and the money is right in front of you—but remember: the wilder it rallies, the nastier it crashes. Retail is euphoric, institutions are quietly unloading, and $7 trillion in cash is waiting for the right moment. Nvidia’s earnings tomorrow could light the fuse. To win this game, you’ve got to jump in fast, exit ruthlessly, and manage your risk. Don’t let the market’s party blind you—make your money, and be the one still smiling at the end. That’s the real victory.
