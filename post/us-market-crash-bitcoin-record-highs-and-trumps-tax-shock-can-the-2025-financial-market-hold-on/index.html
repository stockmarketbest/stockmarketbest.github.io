U.S. Market Crash, Bitcoin Record Highs, and Trump’s Tax Shock: Can the 2025 Financial Market Hold On?

2025-05-23 10 min read

# QQQ # SPY # DIA # .DJI # .SPX # .IXIC # GLD # BTC # trump # stock market # NVDA # TSLA # AAPL # META # GOOGL

Hello everyone, today is Thursday, May 22, 2025. Yesterday’s U.S. stock market plunge rattled nerves across the board. Today, investors staged a timid rebound by bargain-hunting in large-cap tech stocks, managing to stabilize the market just enough. But don’t get too comfortable — the shadow of high debt levels looms large, making this market extremely fragile. The major indexes barely closed flat, with sector movements all over the place. Today, let’s strip back the layers of this chaotic financial market to uncover the hidden opportunities — and landmines.

I. Market Volatility: A Heart-Pounding Ride

1. U.S. Stocks: Buying the Dip or Catching a Falling Knife?

Yesterday’s stock sell-off was brutal, with all three major indexes plunging like a roller coaster. Today, investor sentiment slightly improved, with dip-buyers rushing into familiar big tech names like Apple and Microsoft, dragging the indexes back up a bit. But sentiment remains as fragile as shattered glass. The market closed essentially flat, with sector performance a complete mess — a classic case of “good data, weak conviction.”

There was some relief on the data front: Initial jobless claims came in at 227,000 — better than the expected 230,000 and the previous 229,000. U.S. May PMI flash readings for both manufacturing and services also beat expectations, staying above the 50 boom-bust threshold, suggesting the economy is still breathing. But don't forget, uncertainties around trade policy are still clouding the outlook. The market briefly surged on the data, then quickly fizzled, ending the day in tight range-bound trading — all thunder, no rain.

This bounce likely stems from companies front-running tariff risks. The White House granted a 90-day grace period on tariffs, meaning real stress tests will come with July and August data. With economic figures holding up, the Fed has no reason to cut rates — forget about a June rate cut. Investors, stay grounded: this market is not for the faint of heart.

2. Trump’s Tax Bill: Market Booster or Debt Bomb?

This morning, Trump’s new tax bill barely squeaked through the House with a razor-thin 215–214 vote (1 abstention). Every Democrat opposed it, and even Republicans were deeply divided. Trump personally intervened — calling lawmakers, visiting Capitol Hill, hosting late-night meetings in the Oval Office — and somehow pushed it across the line. His budget office warned: “Any Republican who doesn’t back it is a traitor!” That’s some intense pressure.

What’s in the bill? It extends the 2017 tax cuts, slashes taxes on tips and auto loans, and boosts defense and border security spending — but it also slashes support for food and medical programs for low-income groups. Critics are furious, warning it could add another $3–5 trillion to the debt, pushing the total to a frightening $36.2 trillion over the next decade. The bill now heads to the Senate, with a vote expected between July and August. Until then, markets are likely to remain on edge.

II. Bond Market: U.S. and Japan Tug of War

1. U.S. Treasuries: Putting Out Fires or Pouring Gas?

The tax bill sent Treasury yields surging, with heavy selling in long-term futures. But around noon, aggressive buying came in, pushing prices back up and yields down. Who stepped in? Most likely the Treasury or Fed, directing financial institutions to stabilize the bond market. It worked — for now. But with national debt ballooning, how long can this last?

2. Japanese Bonds: A Global Time Bomb

Over in Japan, 30-year government bond yields spiked to 3.17% — a 25-year high! Rising yields are like giant magnets, pulling capital away from the U.S. and triggering a rush to unwind yen carry trades. We saw this same turbulence last July–August, when U.S. stocks, bonds, and the dollar all got hit. This time, the Fed and Treasury seem smarter, trying to slow yield increases. It’s not the absolute yield that matters — it’s the pace. A slow rise to 5% is fine. A surge in days? That’s a crisis.

III. Stocks and Sectors: One Step from Boom to Bust

1. Solar Stocks: Hot Trade or Retail Trap?

Earlier this week, media reports suggested Trump’s tax bill would reduce — but not eliminate — wind and solar tax credits, and further limit imports of Chinese solar products. That sparked a frenzy in U.S. solar stocks. Sunrun soared 88% in five days, looking ready to take off. But once the bill passed, the sector collapsed. Sunrun gave back all its gains, and solar ETF TAN plunged 7.45%, likely heading back to April lows around $26. Classic case of insiders pumping on good news, dumping on retail investors. With Trump in office, green energy stocks are likely dead money. Wait for the Democrats.

2. DECK vs. Snowflake: Boom and Bust

DECK (Deckers Outdoor) cratered after earnings. Despite beating Q4 revenue and profit estimates, weak current-quarter guidance and no full-year forecast — combined with tariff worries — sent the stock tumbling. Even a bold $2.25 billion buyback couldn’t save it. The stock is down 38% year-to-date and got halved after-hours. A nine-year bull run? Shattered.

Snowflake, on the other hand, rallied post-earnings. Q1 product revenue rose 26%, with EPS of $0.26 beating expectations. RPO came in at $6.7 billion, also strong. Q2 revenue is expected to rise 25%, and the company raised full-year guidance. Since April, the stock is up 70%. But with a forward P/E of 155 — far higher than peers — it's not cheap. They're expanding fast, adding over 400 employees focused on sales and marketing. But competition from Databricks, Microsoft, and Google is intense. Stock-based compensation was 41% of revenue last year — massively dilutive. Still unprofitable five years post-IPO, Snowflake looks like a classic momentum stock. Trade the trend, but beware.

IV. Crypto: Record Highs Hide Risk

1. Bitcoin Hits New High: Bull Market or Bubble?

Bitcoin hit a new all-time high today — euphoria is in the air. But what's driving it? The Wall Street Journal reports that Kraken plans to tokenize U.S. stocks like Apple, Tesla, and Nvidia, as well as ETFs like SPY and GLD, to allow non-U.S. clients to trade them 24/7 — bypassing market closures. Binance tried this in 2021 but regulators shut it down within months. Kraken's plan? It’ll depend on regulatory green lights. This new Bitcoin high is riding on speculation. Retail traders: don’t chase blindly. The risk of being the last one holding the bag is real.

V. Corporate Strategy: Survival Mode for the Giants

1. Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac: IPO Dream Reignited?

Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac shares soared to their highest levels since 2008 after Trump posted that he’s seriously considering taking both public. He said he’ll consult with the Treasury Secretary, Commerce Secretary, and the FHFA head, with decisions coming soon. Markets sniffed a major opportunity. But if these GSEs really IPO, what kind of waves would that send through the housing market?

2. Nike’s Price Hike: Tariff-Induced Survival Tactics

New tariffs have hit the retail sector hard. Nike is reacting by hiking prices $2–10 starting June 1 — a month ahead of the July 8 tariff reactivation. Major sourcing regions like China and Vietnam are affected. Nike also announced it’s returning to direct sales via Amazon after pulling out in 2019, and is expanding in department stores. Will these moves revive sales? That depends on whether consumers are still willing to pay.

VI. Health Insurance: UnitedHealth’s Scandal Spiral

UnitedHealth shares have dropped for two straight days, and insider buying hasn’t stemmed the slide. Media reports claim the company secretly paid nursing homes bonuses to avoid sending seriously ill seniors to hospitals — all to cut costs. UnitedHealth denies the allegations. Still, the government just announced an expanded audit of Medicare Advantage contracts from 2018–2024. That’s another major hit for UnitedHealth and its vendors. This sector is deep in murky waters — brace for more volatility.

VII. Conclusion: Gold in Chaos, But Watch Your Step

The financial market is a tangled mess. Solid economic data is being overshadowed by trade uncertainty and ballooning debt. The bond market hinges on yield speed, not just levels. Stocks are a mixed bag, and crypto innovation is exciting but risky under regulatory scrutiny. Giants are scrambling to adapt, while healthcare is under fire. Where do we go from here? Policy and data will lead the way. Investors: stay sharp, don’t get dazzled by surface-level hype. There’s gold in the chaos — but landmines too. Tread carefully.
